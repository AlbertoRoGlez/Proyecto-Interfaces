  • How do I return in stores a product I have purchased on the web?

    • To return a product purchased on the web to a store, all you have to do is go to the corresponding department of a store and present your sales document. Remember that this document is the "order being shipped" and "order delivered" emails, where the following indication is included:

    "This email is your sales document. If you wish to return any product of your order free of charge, you only have to present it printed or on your cell phone in a store".

    You don't need to bring it printed, you can show it directly from your cell phone.

  • What are the deadlines for returning a product?

    • The following return terms will be counted from the delivery of the order:

    30 calendar days: fashion and sportswear products, accessories, footwear, household goods.

    15 calendar days: albums, disks, figurines.

    Most of the products purchased at Weverse can be returned, as long as they are in perfect condition and unassembled. Including accessories, documentation and packaging, otherwise the good will suffer a depreciation. We remind our customers that it is recommended that the packaging be in perfect condition. There are some exceptions and certain deadlines to be able to make the return.

    Once the goods have been received in our warehouses and the delivery has been verified, we will proceed to refund the amount and shipping costs, according to the method of payment made by the customer, except cash payment, which will be refunded by bank transfer within a period not exceeding 14 calendar days from receipt of the returned goods. The direct cost of returning the goods or service will be borne by the customer as stated in the LGCU.

  • Are there any return costs?

    • Depending on how the return is made. If it is done in stores or through Correos, it is totally free. If it is made in Celeritas, the cost is 2 dollars.

    In case of return of items whose collection is made at home, the direct cost of returning the goods or service will be borne by the customer as stated in the LGCU.
